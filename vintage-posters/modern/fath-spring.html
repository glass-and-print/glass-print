Jacques Fath (1912-1954) was a French fashion designer who was considered one of the three dominant influences on postwar haute couture, the others being Christian Dior and Pierre Balmain. Fath hired a number of young designers as assistants and apprentices, some of which later went on to form their own houses, including Givenchy, Laroche, and Valentino. He dressed the stars; Ava Gardner, Greta Garbo, and Rita Hayworth, who wore a Fath dress for her wedding to Prince Aly Khan.
